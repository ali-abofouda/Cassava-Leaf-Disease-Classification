Project Overview

This project uses deep learning to automatically classify diseases in cassava leaves. Cassava is a major food crop in Africa, and early disease detection is crucial for preventing crop losses and ensuring food security.

We trained a MobileNetV2 model using transfer learning on a dataset of over 21,000 images. The model can identify 5 different conditions: 4 diseases and healthy leaves.

Model Architecture

Base Model: MobileNetV2 (pretrained on ImageNet)

Custom Classifier:

  • Dropout Layer (0.3)
  • Fully Connected Layer (1280 → 256)
  • ReLU Activation
  • Batch Normalization
  • Dropout Layer (0.3)
  • Output Layer (256 → 5 classes)

Training Configuration:

  • Image Size: 224×224
  • Optimizer: Adam (lr=0.0001)
  • LR Scheduler: ReduceLROnPlateau
  • Mixed Precision Training: Enabled

Disease Information

A bacterial disease caused by Xanthomonas axonopodis. Symptoms include angular leaf spots, wilting, and gum exudation from stems.

A viral disease causing yellow/brown streaks on leaves and stems. It can cause severe root necrosis, making the cassava inedible.

A viral disease causing chlorotic (yellow-green) mosaic patterns on leaves and leaf distortion.

The most devastating cassava disease in Africa. Causes leaf curling, mosaic patterns, and severe yield reduction (up to 100% loss in severe cases).

Healthy cassava leaves show no visible disease symptoms. They have uniform green coloration and normal leaf structure.
